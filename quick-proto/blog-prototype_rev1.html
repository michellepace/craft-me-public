margins of things.

Marginalia is an independent journal about the overlooked edges of culture — where architecture meets code, where vinyl meets interface design, where a walk without purpose becomes a philosophy.

Founded in 2024, Marginalia began as a series of letters between four writers who couldn't stop noticing connections between seemingly unrelated fields. Elena, an architect turned essayist. James, a software engineer who writes about sound. Maren, a biologist fascinated by design. And Luca, a typographer who thinks everything is political.

We don't cover news. We don't chase trends. Instead, we look for the quiet patterns — the ways a bookshop's layout mirrors a database schema, the reasons a font feels authoritarian, the ecology growing between pavement cracks.

Our Writers

Elena Marchetti writes about architecture, space, and the philosophy of walking. Based in Rome, she previously worked as an architect at Studio Zaha before turning to writing full-time. Her essay collection Thresholds was longlisted for the Orwell Prize.

James Okafor is a Lagos-born, London-based software engineer and music writer. He explores the intersections of code, sound, and politics. His newsletter "Compiled" has 12,000 subscribers.

Maren Ishida is a field biologist and cultural critic based in Kyoto. She writes about ecology, design, and the intelligence of non-human systems. She runs a small urban rewilding project.

Luca Ferreira is a typographer, designer, and essayist from São Paulo. He is interested in how visual systems shape power and meaning. He teaches typography at the Royal College of Art.
